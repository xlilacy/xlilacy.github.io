title: The Rainbow's Perspective
---

I arch above the drowning scene,
my spectrum split through storm and spray,
helpless as prisms of light can be,
watching feathers drift away.
I who am born of sun and rain
can only paint his falling form
in shifting hues—his struggles stain
my violet depths, my amber warm.
Through indigo, he sinks from sight,
past blue that once was his domain.
My green reflects lost forest flight,
while yellow catches his last strain.
In orange depths, his wings unfold
like autumn leaves in water-dance.
Through deepest red, I watch him fold
into the dark's final romance.
How cruel that I must bend and shine,
while he descends through every shade
that makes my being. Line by line,
I watch his colors bleed and fade.
I've decorated many graves
with paths of light through heaven's tears,
but never thought I'd mark the waves
where sky-born glory disappears.
My colors fade as evening nears,
but still I hold my vigil high—
a bridge between his two frontiers:
the sea below, his former sky.
