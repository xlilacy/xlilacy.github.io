title: Morning Glory
---

Light spills across linoleum,
catching shards of yesterday's favorite mug—
the one with paint-chipped flowers
you brought from your mother's house.
I watch dust motes dance
in the beam between us,
each particle spinning its own
silent revolution around our kitchen.
Your hands sweep fragments
into a neat pile of what was,
and I notice how the broken edges
catch morning like prisms,
throwing rainbows on your forearms
as you work. Even in this,
there is something of love—
the careful way you gather pieces,
how you pause to examine each one
before letting it go, like memories
we've collected and scattered,
glinting as they fall.
