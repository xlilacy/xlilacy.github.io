title: Emotional Source
---

The morning light betrays
what darkness kept—your empty chair,
the cup you always used, now scattered
across our kitchen floor like broken vows.
I should sweep these pieces up, but
I've been staring at them for hours,
watching sunlight creep across porcelain,
wondering how many mornings I wasted,
not memorizing the way your hands
cradled warmth, how your lips left
half-moons of lipstick on the rim—
now just another shard that cuts.
They say morning heals, but each dawn
feels like fresh betrayal, pouring light
into corners where your shadow should be,
illuminating all the spaces you've abandoned.
I leave the pieces where they fell,
let them catch the sun like tears
I cannot shed. Some things, once broken,
should be allowed to stay that way.
