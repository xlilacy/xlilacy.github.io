title: A Fairy's Lament
---

These mortals shuffle past my mushroom ring,
unseeing, wrapped in metal shells that ping—
while I, who danced with stars before their birth,
now hide beneath dead leaves upon the earth.
My wings, once gossamer and lightning-quick,
have dulled to match the morning's heavy mist.
Where once I skimmed through gardens wild and free,
I rest on withered flowers, tentatively.
A thousand years of midnight revelry,
of stealing children's teeth and brewing tea
from moondrops caught in spidersilk and dew—
now all my magic barely glimmers through.
The humans build their towers to the sky,
while underneath, our hollow hills run dry.
My sisters fade like twilight, one by one,
their sparkle dimmed by tungsten, neon, sun.
I weave my spells with arthritic fingers now,
my mischief slower, though I still know how
to turn milk sour, tangle dreams in knots—
but oh, how even chaos sometimes rots.
Yet still I guard this last enchanted space,
this circle where the wild and mundane embrace.
Perhaps I'll fade when final magic dies,
or learn, like mortals, how to compromise.
