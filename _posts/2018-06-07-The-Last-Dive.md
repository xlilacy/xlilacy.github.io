title: The Last Dive
---

Once I split clouds with my wingtips,
carved highways through storm-dark skies—
now water fills my hollow bones
where air should sing its passage.
These wings that caught thermals
like love letters from the sun
drag me deeper, waterlogged
and strange in this liquid world.
The surface ripples above,
a mirror of the sky I knew,
distorting sunlight into ribbons
that mock my downward spiral.
I try to beat against the blue
as I once beat against the wind,
but water thinks in different rhythms
than the ones my body learned.
My feathers, once precise as knives,
now float like dying stars around me,
each one a memory of height,
of diving because I chose to.
The pressure builds in pearl-white bubbles
from my beak—my last held piece of sky
escaping upward while I sink,
teaching gravity new ways to betray.
Through salt-stung eyes, I watch
my shadow grow beneath me,
spreading like spilled ink
across the ocean floor.
How strange, to find another kind
of flight in falling—
to learn too late that drowning
is just flying in reverse.
